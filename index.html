01 — Overview

Three things worth knowing before the detail.

Across both platforms, the same categories keep winning. The work ahead is less about spending more, and more about moving the budget that already exists.

01

Amazon is healthy where the money sits, leaky around it.

Sponsored Products runs a 27% ACOS and brings in 86% of sales. The waste sits around that core: good campaigns capped by their own budgets, winning targets bidding too low, and a tail of expensive targets quietly funding themselves at everyone else's expense.

02

BigBasket makes money, but the budget sits on the wrong products.

Close to half of spend went into Vinegar, which ran a 69% ACOS, while Jalapeno and Gherkin SKUs returned an 8–12x ROAS on next to no spend. This is a reallocation problem, not a budget problem.

Vinegar — 69% ACOS Jalapeno — 8–12x ROAS Gherkins — 8–12x ROAS
03

The winners repeat across both channels.

Jalapeno and Gherkins sit at the top on Amazon and on BigBasket. Whatever else changes, these two categories should hold the line on budget across every channel.

02 — Amazon

A mature account, converting demand it already has.

Five read-outs from the account snapshot, and what each one actually means for where NEO Foods stands today.

03 — Growth Opportunities

Five moves, in the order they'll pay back.

None of these require new budget. They redirect what's already being spent toward what's already working.

01

Scale Product Targeting beyond current coverage

PT campaigns consistently deliver some of the strongest returns across Jalapeno, Gherkins, and Paprika.

Opportunity — expand PT coverage across high-volume competitor ASINs and complementary products to capture incremental category demand.
02

Convert Discovery campaigns into dedicated Scale campaigns

Several Auto campaigns are delivering strong sales and efficiency — the winning search terms have already been found.

Opportunity — extract high-converting search terms into dedicated Exact campaigns with Top-of-Search support, to improve rank capture and conversion.
03

Build category leadership around Hero portfolios

Jalapeno, Gherkins, Olives, and Paprika top-perform irrespective of targeting type.

Opportunity — shift from SKU-level growth to category-level dominance across search, competitor, and remarketing campaigns.
04

Strengthen full-funnel advertising

Sponsored Products drives the majority of sales today, but the funnel above it is underused.

Opportunity — use SB (Collection Ads), SBV, and SD strategically to build reach, category awareness, and assist organic rank.
05

Leverage AMC audiences for custom targeting

Introduce audience-based campaigns using AMC insights — product viewers, cart abandoners, category shoppers, and cross-category purchasers — to improve acquisition efficiency and drive incremental conversions.

Priority Matrix

Ranked by where the next rupee should go.

1 Free budget trapped in inefficient targets A meaningful share of spend sits on targets above 50% ACOS. Rationalizing these unlocks budget for proven drivers without spending more overall.
2 Raise budget on winning campaigns Several profitable SP campaigns run below ideal budget coverage despite healthy ACOS — missed visibility, missed sales.
3 Scale proven Product Targeting structures PT remains one of the strongest-performing targeting types in the account, particularly within Jalapeno and Gherkins — more headroom to grow.
4 Prioritize Hero SKU investment Revenue stays concentrated in a handful of products while some heavily-funded SKUs underperform. Tighter focus on Hero SKUs lifts overall productivity.
5 Strengthen New-to-Brand acquisition The account converts well but acquires narrowly. SBV, SD Prospecting, and AMC audiences can unlock incremental demand.
6 Improve campaign governance Naming inconsistencies and product-level reporting gaps make tracking and decisions harder than they need to be as the account scales.
Audit Conclusion — Amazon

The account has already established strong campaign coverage and identified its key growth drivers across Product Targeting, Auto, and category-led campaigns. The next phase should focus on strengthening category leadership within core portfolios, expanding competitor and audience-based targeting through AMC, and maximizing visibility for high-contributing products through strategic budget allocation and full-funnel support.

Keyword & Demand Analysis

  • Jalapeno — strongest keyword cluster, 15.6K sales at a 21.5x ROAS. The primary demand driver on the platform.

05 — Roadmap

Strategic recommendations, in sequence.

What to fix now on BigBasket, and what to build once the short-term reallocation is done.

Short-Term
  • Increase investment behind Jalapeno, Olive, and Gherkin — already over 60% of total ad sales.
  • Reallocate budget from low-contributing SKUs toward Red Jalapeno, Kimchi, Whole Gherkins, and Baby Gherkins.
  • Review pricing, promotions, and content for White Vinegar and Apple Cider Vinegar before scaling further.
Medium-Term
  • Move beyond generic phrase targeting — build dedicated campaigns around proven converting keywords and categories.
  • Build category-specific campaign structures for Jalapenos, Gherkins, Olives, and Vinegars.
  • Introduce Hero SKU prioritization so budget concentrates on products with strong sales velocity and profitability.
